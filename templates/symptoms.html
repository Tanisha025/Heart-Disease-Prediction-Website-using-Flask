Symptoms of Heart Disease

Recognizing the symptoms of heart disease is crucial for early intervention. Common symptoms include:

  • Chest pain or discomfort (angina)
  • Shortness of breath, especially during physical activity or exertion
  • Fatigue or weakness, often unexplained
  • Rapid or irregular heartbeat (arrhythmia)
  • Dizziness or fainting
  • Swelling in the legs, ankles, or abdomen (edema)
  • Unexplained weight gain
  • Persistent coughing or wheezing
  • Discomfort or pain in the upper body, including arms, back, neck, jaw, or stomach
  • Nausea or loss of appetite
  • Sweating, often cold sweats
  • Sleep disturbances, such as difficulty sleeping or waking up short of breath
